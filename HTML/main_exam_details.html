Your Exam Guide Starts Here

Find detailed information on eligibility, exam patterns, and preparation tips for top competitive and entrance exams in India.

JEE Main

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Should have passed Class 12th or equivalent examination.
  • There is no age limit for the candidates.
  • Candidates can appear for the exam consecutively for 3 years.

Exam Pattern

The exam consists of two papers, Paper 1 for B.E./B.Tech and Paper 2 for B.Arch/B.Planning. It includes multiple-choice and numerical answer type questions.

Preparation

Focus on strong foundational concepts in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Practice mock tests regularly to manage time effectively and improve speed.

NEET-UG

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
  • Passed Class 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English.
  • Minimum aggregate marks vary by category.

Exam Pattern

A single paper with questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Botany & Zoology). The exam is held in offline mode (pen and paper).

Preparation

Thoroughly study NCERT textbooks for all subjects. Solve previous year's question papers to understand the exam format and frequently asked topics.

UPSC Civil Services

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Holds a bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
  • Age between 21 and 32 years (with relaxations for reserved categories).

Exam Pattern

The exam is conducted in three stages: Prelims (Objective), Mains (Descriptive), and an Interview.

Preparation

Stay updated with current affairs by reading newspapers daily. A holistic study plan that covers General Studies, an optional subject, and regular answer writing practice is essential.

CAT

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must hold a bachelor's degree with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved categories).
  • Final-year students of a bachelor's degree are also eligible to apply.

Exam Pattern

A computer-based test with three sections: Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR), and Quantitative Aptitude (QA).

Preparation

Focus on improving speed and accuracy. Practice different types of questions and analyze mock tests to identify your strengths and weaknesses.

JEE Advanced

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must be among the top 2,50,000 JEE Main qualified candidates.
  • Should have passed Class 12th with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Only two attempts are allowed for this exam.

Exam Pattern

Consists of two papers (Paper 1 and Paper 2), both with multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and numerical answer type questions with complex marking schemes.

Preparation

The exam tests deep conceptual understanding. Focus on problem-solving, analytical thinking, and a strong grip on theoretical principles.

WBJEE

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Passed or appearing for Class 12th or its equivalent.
  • Minimum age of 17 years as of December 31st of the admission year.

Exam Pattern

Consists of two papers: Paper-I (Mathematics) and Paper-II (Physics & Chemistry). Both are multiple-choice question papers.

Preparation

Focus on the syllabus as prescribed by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board (WBJEEB). Practice previous year papers to understand question trends.

SSC CGL

Official Website

Eligibility

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Hold a bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
  • Age varies between 18-32 years depending on the post.

Exam Pattern

The exam is conducted in two tiers: Tier-I (a common objective exam) and Tier-II (consisting of multiple papers for specific roles).

Preparation

Strengthen your skills in Quantitative Aptitude, General Intelligence & Reasoning, English Comprehension, and General Awareness. Consistent practice is key.
